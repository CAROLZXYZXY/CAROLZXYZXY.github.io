In Memory of Thanksgiving 2018

11/22/2018

As thanksgiving comes, I want to express my gratitude through this post to relieve my guilt. Rada asked me yesterday what I am happiest about the past year, and my response is that that I could be exposed to cutting-edge research in this field.

Tracing back this line, I want to thank my advisor Rada and Jag. When I was under the worst condition this February (no internship, no research lab), Rada sincerely accepted my application to her lab. From that time on, I started my fantastic journey in NLP. Even when I feel I don't want to continue project LEAP this September, she gave me strong support towards my decision to quit the project. She always encourages me to do what I'm interested in, and provides me, an undergraduate, a lot of opportunities to discuss research. I also gain chances of meeting other people in academia, CCB from Upenn, Graham Neubig from CMU, etc. She is a fantastic advisor, and I learn a lot from her not only about research, but also lifestyle. I remember her word on the upside of stress, her comment on Appendix of 1984, which gives me endless strength. Jag provided me a simulation environment of being a PhD Student during the summer. And from that time on, I decided to pursue a PhD degree after my bachelor journey. I clearly remembered the first time I met with Jag, even without saying hi, he directly asked whether I want to pursue a PhD. Maybe this question would change my life, forever.

Tracing back more, I'm grateful to Shiyu Zhang. She never got tired of giving me recognition when I was inconfident. I couldn't imagine our friendship from Spanish Winter Program could last so long, and so dense, until now. More importantly, she encouraged me to go to Jag's group, where I came across my fantastic mentor Nikita. As Jag and Rada provided me the best research environment, Nikita let me here by person. Thank you for her continuous care, no matter she was on campus, or during internship. I clearly know that I'm quite bothering, and I have been consuming a lot of her time in helping me things that isn't in the scope of research. Even recently my mind crashes, and I feel continuously hopeless and low, she never got tired of relieving me and cheering me up. I feel sorry that I rarely act positively towards her encouraging word, and I beg her forgiveness for my childish action and thoughts. I don’t intend to do so. I'm also grateful to this special type friendship, between an Indian PhD student and a Chinese undergraduate student, which I could never imagine before this happens. In this phase, thank you God for letting me experience the tiny surprise.

Recently, I got a lot of trouble in gaining the courage of facing my day, my year and my life. One of the worst feeling I have is guilty. Looking at my peers, I really feel I’m the fortunate one in a lot of aspects. Because of that, I'm even more grateful to all the things I already have, sincerely.

Most importantly, I want to thank myself, my body and my mind. Me myself should be the strongest support for this life. Under no circumstances I could give up. Happy Thanksgiving!
